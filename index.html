90-Day Playbook

Three moves I'd
explore first

Hypotheses, not promises. Subject to what I learn in week one.

01

Audit how buyers actually describe the problem

Before writing a word of positioning, I'd interview 6-8 Escape customers to map their vocabulary — how they described the pain before finding Escape, what convinced them, what language they used internally to justify the buy. The gap between their language and Escape's current messaging is where the category narrative lives.

Output: Messaging framework anchored in buyer language, not internal product vocabulary
02

Build two distinct persona narratives: Security Leader vs. Security Engineer

These audiences have different fears. The engineer cares about false positives, CI/CD friction, and coverage. The leader cares about board-level exposure, compliance risk, and headcount ROI. Escape's product serves both — but the copy needs to speak to each without flattening them into one generic "security team" persona.

Output: Two persona playbooks feeding into sales decks, battlecards, and ad copy
03

Turn the competitor comparison pages into a conversion asset

Escape already publishes head-to-head comparisons with StackHawk, Bright, Invicti, and others. But comparison pages on the blog convert differently than a battlecard a rep can use in a live discovery call. I'd build the bridge between those written comparisons and a sales enablement system reps can actually deploy.

Output: Competitive battlecard system tied to common objections and displacement scenarios
